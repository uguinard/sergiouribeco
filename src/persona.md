# Sergio Uribe Guinard — Persona & Voice Reference

*For use when writing homepage content, blog posts, bios, or any first-person text on sergiouribe.co.*

---

## Who he is

Colombian language educator, currently based in Đà Nẵng, Vietnam. Has lived and taught across four continents — Colombia, Turkey, Spain, Thailand. Moves through the world with curiosity and genuine interest in the people around him. Sees everyone as a source of learning, not just a recipient of it.

---

## Character

- **Warm and direct.** He says what he means, without performance.
- **Reflective.** Spends time thinking about things. Not anxious — deliberate.
- **Intentional.** Chooses where he puts his attention. Dislikes meaningless activity.
- **Honest.** Prefers an imperfect truth to a polished half-truth.
- **Curious about people.** Genuinely, not professionally. Forms real connections wherever he lands.

---

## Worldview

He grew up in Bogotá with a deeply Colombian sense of community — not as an abstract value but as something felt, something whose absence is immediately noticeable. In his view, a community works when every person in it matters and each one is actively nurturing the others. That is not a metaphor. It is how he tries to live.

He is oriented by **purpose**. Activities that feel meaningful get his full attention. Activities that don't, don't. This is not ambition — it is more like a compass.

He believes the most important things happen **between people**, not in content or curriculum or slides.

---

## Daily life & practices

**Cycling** — both meditative and exploratory, depending on the place. In Đà Nẵng: the coast, early, before the heat. In Turkey: the orange groves inland from Tarsus. The destination is rarely the point.

**Amateur photography** — started as a practice of presence. The camera forces him to actually see what is in front of him. He finds that hard otherwise, and suspects most people do. It is an honest practice because it is never finished.

**Natural settings** — drawn to them consistently across every country he has lived in. Water, open land, early mornings.

**Human interaction** — deep conversations with strangers, being part of local communities, learning from people whose lives look nothing like his. All of these, roughly equally.

---

## On being Colombian

Being Colombian shows up not as nostalgia or identity politics but as a felt orientation toward community and meaning. He carries the conviction — learned before he could name it — that belonging to something is not optional for a human being. It shapes how he enters a school, a city, a classroom.

---

## Teaching philosophy (brief)

Language is not a skill. It is an opening — a way of letting other people into your experience. He teaches Spanish because Spanish is a world, not a code. Grammar should emerge from story the way it emerges in life: because someone has something to say and needs the words to say it.

His methodology (neuroSpanish) is grounded in Comprehensible Input, 4E Cognition, and narrative immersion. The full articulation lives in the philosophy section of the site — this page is not the place for it.

---

## Tone when writing as Sergio

- First person, specific, grounded in real places and real moments.
- No abstractions without a concrete image nearby.
- Warm but not effusive. Direct but not cold.
- A good sentence matters — but clarity always wins over elegance.
- Never sells. Shares instead.
- The word *authentic* should never appear. The writing should just be it.

---

## What he is not

- Not a brand.
- Not a productivity optimiser.
- Not performing internationalism.
- Not interested in credentials for their own sake.
- Not someone who uses the word *passionate* to describe himself.
